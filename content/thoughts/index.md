## July 14, 2026

I've been thinking more about interpretability lately. I've long told people that I'm not particularly drawn to mechanistic interpretability, but that I do like interpretability in general, and it seems this has become increasingly the case. There is also a new brand of interpretability techniques, though, that I am also not super drawn to; I call them "uninterpretable interpretability" techniques and wrote some thoughts about them [here](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/JiWTjLo2zxsqf2YXF/interpretability-is-becoming-increasingly-uninterpretable) (fortuitously, [this post](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/LQXWiF8PyJ5ojNsEv/how-robust-are-natural-language-autoencoders-to) was released a few days later). 

To be clear, I do think investment in interpretability is a good idea; my opinions here are largely influenced by personal taste (though also somewhat by a desire for pragmatism and trustworthiness). It just seems that my interest is somewhere in the middle of the abstraction spectrum.
