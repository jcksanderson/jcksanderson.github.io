Hi! My name is Jack and I'm a (rising) fourth-year undergrad at the University of Chicago studying statistics and computer science. I'm largely interested in AI safety, security, and interpretability, with the general goal of making AI systems more transparent and less bad. Read some of my random current thoughts [here]({{< ref "thoughts/index.md" >}}).

This summer I am a visiting researcher at EPFL and working part-time for [Gray Swan AI](https://www.grayswan.ai/). Most recently I partook in some work on [iterative LLM finetuning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01130) and sequential data poisoning, and last summer I created an [AI Security Guide](https://xlabaisecurity.com/) with [Zephy Roe](https://zephaniahdev.com/). Further in the past I investigated [compute-algorithm interactions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.04075), which was featured on Epoch AI's [Gradient Updates](https://epoch.ai/gradient-updates/how-fast-can-algorithms-advance-capabilities).
